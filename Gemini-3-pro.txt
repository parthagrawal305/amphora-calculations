How this UI solves your user's problem:

Immediate Value Validation: The developer sees "194% ROI" (or similar) immediately because the defaults are set to your successful case study. This builds trust before they even touch a slider.

Granularity without Clutter:

I grouped Ad Type into a specific sub-section. Instead of just asking for "Revenue per ad," I gave them the levers they actually have: CPM (passive views) and CPC (clicks) + CTR. This is much more realistic for a publisher trying to estimate real earnings.

I included Inventory Split: The code assumes a mix of CPM and CPC ads (set to 65/35 based on your PDF), but the user can tweak the prices to simulate "What if I only had CPM ads?".

The "Per Conversation" Metric: In the breakdown section, I added a pill badge: Per Conversation: $0.003 profit. This directly answers the "Unit Economics" question highlighted in the PDF.

Developer Logic:

I used Math.floor for ad slots logic. If a conversation is 6 messages and frequency is 1 ad per 10 messages, the calculator correctly predicts 0 ads, showing the user that their conversation length is too short for their frequency setting. This creates a "Aha!" moment where they realize they need to adjust the frequency to monetize shorter chats.
